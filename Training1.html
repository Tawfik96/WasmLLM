LoRA

LoRA (Low-Rank Adaptation) is a parameter-efficient technique for training large language models (LLMs) in a self-supervised manner across various types of data. It is especially effective for fine-tuning models on specific downstream tasks using your own dataset.

USE LoRA

RHLF

Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback is a method for improving LLMs by integrating human feedback into the training process. This approach allows models to refine their responses based on evaluations of desired outputs, ensuring that generated content closely aligns with human values and preferences, ideal for user-centric applications.

USE RHLF

DPO

DPO customizes LLMs to align with specific user preferences. The model is trained using a prompt, a preferred response, and a dispreferred response. The model learns to favor responses that are more aligned with the desired outcomes and reduce the generation of undesirable responses. This technique is advantageous for applications requiring highly personalized and aligned content generation.

USE DPO
